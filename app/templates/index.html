Predicting the Genre of a Song

A data science project by AI Camp students

See our work below

What does the project do?

In our dataset, there is information on tempo, danceability, energy, loudness, etc... that correlate with each other in interesting ways. Our goal will be to predict the genre of a song using this information. Looking at some characteristics of the dataset, half of the songs are categorized as rap and the other half are classical.

Machine Learning


KNN: Explanation

KNN works by first choosing the number of neighbors, and then for each data point calculating the euclidean distance between K number of neighbors, and then categorizing that data point into whatever category the majority of the closest neighboring points are. In our problem, since the numeric values for rap and classical are significantly different, the categories of each data points neighbors are most likely going to be the same category as the data point itself.

KNN: Results

Here we have a confusion matrix with the results of the knn model applied on our testing dataset. A confusion matrix for a classification problem tells us the number of correct and incorrect predictions for every class. KNN performed exceptionally well on classifying the genre of a song. Looking at the confusion matrix below, we can see that their were only 2 incorrect predictions.

The accuracy for our KNN model was 98.4%

Decision Tree Classifier: Explanation

Because our problem is a classification problem, using a Decision Tree Classifier is always an option. A Decision Tree Classifier works by learning simple decision rules from the training data. There is a root in the tree, which is the first question in the tree. In the root the DTC compares the root attribute with the data point's attribute. Based on this comparison, the DTC follows the corresponding branch and eventually classifies the data point. In our problem, there may be many layers in DTC with example questions such as Is the loudness less than .3? DTC uses these questions to help classify data points.

Decision Tree Classifier: Results

Looking at the confusion matrix below, the DCT model performed perfectly.

For total accuracy, the DCT performed at 100% accuracy in classification.

Logistic Regression Classifier: Explanation

Logistic Regression is another supervised classification algorithm than we can use in this problem. The model builds a regression model to predict the probability that a given data entry belongs to the category numbered as “1”. Just like Linear regression assumes that the data follows a linear function, Logistic regression models the data using the sigmoid function. In our problem, we are using binomial logistic regression because we only have two labels, 1 or 0, rap or classical music.

Logistic Regression Classifier: Results

Looking at our confusion matrix below, the Logistic Regression also performed well on classifying rap and classical music.

For total accuracy, the LRC performed at 99.2% accuracy in classification.

Conclusion


In conclusion, all three models that we picked perform really well for the task of predicting the genre of a song. The best model was the Decision Tree Classifier, having perfect predictions. All of them can prove our thesis that rap is very different from classical music and that they can easily be labled based on data about thir musical features. One way we can improve our model is to play around with different subsets of feature data and see which subset gives us a higher accuracy. Another way we can imporve our model is to perfrom cross validation or grid search in order to see what parameters are best for our models. Something we can further do expand this project is to compare another genre of music. We can change our model into a multi label classifier by adding another genre to predict. Another possible advancement of this project is to conduct NLP analysis on the lyrics of songs using the Genuis API.

So what?

So what is the significance of our project? Humans are able to tell apart different musical genres just by listening with their ears. This project has shown as that computers can do the same thing, without even needing ears or even needing to listen to the song.
